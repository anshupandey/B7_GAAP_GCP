# FDA Compliance Guidelines for Medical Supplies

**Document ID:** POL-FDA-002  
**Effective Date:** March 1, 2025  
**Review Cycle:** Semi-Annual  
**Owner:** Regulatory Affairs Division

## 1. Purpose

This document outlines MedSupply Corp's requirements for FDA compliance across all medical supply categories. All warehouses, suppliers, and distribution partners must adhere to these guidelines.

## 2. Product Classification & Registration

### Class I (Low Risk)
Examples: adhesive bandages, exam gloves, tongue depressors. General controls apply. Most are exempt from 510(k) premarket notification. Annual FDA registration required.

### Class II (Moderate Risk)
Examples: blood pressure monitors, pulse oximeters, powered wheelchairs. Require 510(k) clearance. Subject to special controls, performance standards, and post-market surveillance.

### Class III (High Risk)
Examples: implantable devices, life-sustaining equipment. Require Premarket Approval (PMA). Not currently in MedSupply Corp's product portfolio but may be added pending board approval.

## 3. Storage & Handling Requirements

### General Storage
- All products stored in climate-controlled environments (60–77°F / 15–25°C)
- Humidity maintained below 60% RH
- FIFO (First In, First Out) inventory rotation mandatory
- Lot tracking enabled for all pharmaceutical and Class II products

### Pharmaceutical Storage
- Controlled substances in locked, access-restricted areas with electronic audit trails
- Cold chain products per Cold Chain Temperature SOP (POL-CC-008)
- Temperature monitoring with automated alerts (deviation > ±2°C triggers investigation)

### Equipment Storage
- Original manufacturer packaging until distribution
- Electrostatic discharge (ESD) protection for electronic equipment
- Calibration records maintained for all measurement devices

## 4. Labeling Requirements

All products distributed by MedSupply Corp must carry:
1. Product name and description
2. Manufacturer name and address
3. Lot/batch number
4. Expiration date (where applicable)
5. Storage condition instructions
6. FDA registration number
7. UDI (Unique Device Identifier) for Class II and above

Labels must be in English. Bilingual labels (English/Spanish) required for US distribution.

## 5. Traceability & Recall Readiness

- Full chain-of-custody documentation from manufacturer to end customer
- Lot-level traceability: ability to trace any product to supplier, shipment, and warehouse within 4 hours
- Annual mock recall drill required (must achieve 95%+ product location accuracy)
- Actual recall procedure documented in Product Recall Procedure (POL-RCL-005)

## 6. Audit & Inspection Preparedness

- FDA inspection readiness review conducted quarterly
- Document retention: minimum 3 years for distribution records, 7 years for complaint files
- All non-conformances logged in the CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action) system within 48 hours
- Supplier audit schedule maintained by Quality Assurance (annual for Tier-1, semi-annual for Tier-2)
