# Warehouse Temperature Control & Cold Chain SOP

**Document ID:** SOP-CC-008  
**Effective Date:** March 15, 2025  
**Review Cycle:** Semi-Annual  
**Owner:** Warehouse Operations & Quality Assurance

## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines the temperature monitoring, control, and response procedures for pharmaceutical and temperature-sensitive medical supplies stored in MedSupply Corp warehouses. It ensures compliance with FDA 21 CFR Part 211 (drug storage) and USP <1079> (good storage practices).

## 2. Temperature Zones

### Ambient / Controlled Room Temperature (CRT)
- **Range:** 20–25°C (68–77°F), with excursions permitted to 15–30°C (59–86°F)
- **Products:** Most pharmaceuticals, medical devices, PPE, consumables
- **Monitoring:** Continuous digital sensors, logged every 15 minutes
- **Alert threshold:** Outside 15–30°C for more than 30 minutes

### Refrigerated (Cold Storage)
- **Range:** 2–8°C (36–46°F)
- **Products:** Insulin, certain biologics, vaccines (if applicable), temperature-sensitive reagents
- **Monitoring:** Continuous sensors with battery backup, logged every 5 minutes
- **Alert threshold:** Outside 2–8°C for more than 15 minutes

### Frozen
- **Range:** -25 to -10°C (-13 to 14°F)
- **Products:** Certain biologics, tissue samples, specialty pharmaceuticals
- **Monitoring:** Continuous sensors with redundant backup, logged every 5 minutes
- **Alert threshold:** Outside range for more than 10 minutes

## 3. Monitoring Equipment

### Required Equipment Per Zone
1. Minimum 2 calibrated digital temperature sensors per storage area (primary + backup)
2. Sensors calibrated annually by accredited laboratory (NIST-traceable)
3. Continuous monitoring system with cloud-based data logging
4. Automated alert system: SMS + email to on-duty warehouse personnel and Quality on-call
5. Backup power: UPS for monitoring systems (minimum 4-hour battery life)
6. Generator backup for refrigeration units (automatic transfer within 30 seconds)

### Calibration Schedule
- Digital sensors: Annual calibration, quarterly verification against reference thermometer
- Reference thermometer: Annual calibration by NIST-accredited laboratory
- Data loggers: Battery replacement every 6 months; full replacement every 3 years

## 4. Temperature Excursion Response

### Step 1: Immediate Response (within 15 minutes of alert)
1. On-duty personnel verify the reading (confirm sensor is not malfunctioning)
2. If confirmed excursion: check HVAC/refrigeration system status
3. If equipment failure: activate backup cooling; move products to alternate zone if necessary

### Step 2: Assessment (within 2 hours)
1. Quality Specialist assesses affected inventory:
   - Identify all lots in the affected zone
   - Review product stability data for temperature tolerance
   - Determine if excursion is within manufacturer-specified limits
2. If within limits: document excursion, return products to active inventory
3. If outside limits or unknown: place affected lots on QUALITY HOLD

### Step 3: Investigation (within 24 hours)
1. Root cause investigation initiated
2. Maintenance team inspects equipment; repair or replace
3. If systemic issue: escalate to Facilities Management and VP Operations

### Step 4: Disposition (within 72 hours)
1. Quality Manager reviews all held lots
2. Decision options: release, quarantine pending manufacturer consultation, or destroy
3. All decisions documented with justification
4. If product destroyed: regulatory reporting per POL-RCL-005 if product had been distributed

## 5. Receiving & Shipping

### Inbound Shipments
1. Temperature-sensitive shipments must include a temperature data logger
2. Receiving staff check logger upon arrival; any excursion during transit triggers rejection or hold
3. Products transferred to appropriate temperature zone within 30 minutes of receipt
4. Cold chain continuity verified: time from manufacturer's cold storage to MedSupply cold storage documented

### Outbound Shipments
1. Insulated shipping containers with gel packs (refrigerated) or dry ice (frozen)
2. Temperature logger included in every cold chain shipment
3. Shipping routes optimized for maximum 48-hour transit (refrigerated) or 24-hour (frozen)
4. Summer shipping protocol (June–September): additional gel packs, morning-only pickups, express shipping only

## 6. Documentation & Record Retention

- Continuous temperature logs: retained for 3 years (digital)
- Excursion investigation reports: retained for 7 years
- Calibration certificates: retained for 5 years
- Equipment maintenance records: retained for the life of the equipment + 3 years
- All records must be readily accessible for FDA inspection within 24 hours of request
