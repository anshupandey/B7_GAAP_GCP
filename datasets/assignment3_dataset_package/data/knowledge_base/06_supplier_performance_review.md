# Supplier Performance Review Framework

**Document ID:** POL-SPR-006  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Review Cycle:** Annual  
**Owner:** Strategic Procurement & Quality Assurance

## 1. Purpose

This framework defines how MedSupply Corp evaluates supplier performance on an ongoing basis. It establishes KPIs, scoring methodology, and escalation procedures for underperforming suppliers.

## 2. Review Schedule

- **Monthly:** Automated scorecard generated from operational data (no manual input required)
- **Quarterly:** Procurement + Quality joint review of all Tier-1 and Tier-2 suppliers
- **Annual:** Comprehensive review including on-site audit results, contract renewal recommendation
- **Ad-hoc:** Triggered by any critical quality event or delivery failure

## 3. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

### 3.1 Quality Score (40% weight)
- **Defect Rate:** Percentage of units rejected during incoming quality inspection
  - Target: < 2% (Excellent), 2–5% (Acceptable), 5–8% (Warning), > 8% (Critical)
- **CAPA Responsiveness:** Average days to complete corrective actions
  - Target: < 10 days (Excellent), 10–20 days (Acceptable), > 20 days (Warning)
- **Recall Involvement:** Number of recall events in trailing 12 months
  - Target: 0 (Excellent), 1 (Acceptable), 2+ (Critical)

### 3.2 Delivery Score (30% weight)
- **On-Time Delivery Rate:** Percentage of orders delivered by committed date
  - Target: > 95% (Excellent), 90–95% (Acceptable), 85–90% (Warning), < 85% (Critical)
- **Lead Time Consistency:** Standard deviation of actual vs committed lead time
  - Target: < 2 days (Excellent), 2–5 days (Acceptable), > 5 days (Warning)
- **Order Completeness:** Percentage of orders delivered in full quantity
  - Target: > 98% (Excellent), 95–98% (Acceptable), < 95% (Warning)

### 3.3 Commercial Score (20% weight)
- **Price Competitiveness:** Compared to market benchmark and competing suppliers
- **Invoice Accuracy:** Percentage of invoices matching PO terms
- **Payment Term Compliance:** Adherence to agreed payment terms

### 3.4 Compliance Score (10% weight)
- **Certification Currency:** All required certifications valid and up-to-date
- **Regulatory Standing:** No FDA warning letters, import alerts, or sanctions
- **Audit Findings:** Number of open critical/major findings from latest audit

## 4. Composite Scoring

Overall Score = (Quality × 0.40) + (Delivery × 0.30) + (Commercial × 0.20) + (Compliance × 0.10)

Scoring scale: 1 (Critical) to 5 (Excellent)

### Tier Implications
- Score 4.0–5.0: Maintain current tier; eligible for volume increase
- Score 3.0–3.9: Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) issued; reviewed in 90 days
- Score 2.0–2.9: Tier downgrade; order volume reduced by 50%; PIP with 60-day deadline
- Score below 2.0: Immediate suspension of new POs; contract termination review

## 5. Escalation Procedures

### Defect Rate Exceeds 5%
1. Immediate notification to supplier with request for root cause analysis (5 business days)
2. Incoming inspection rate increased to 100% for next 3 shipments
3. Procurement Manager and Quality Manager joint call with supplier within 10 business days
4. If not resolved within 30 days: formal PIP issued with specific targets and timeline

### Defect Rate Exceeds 8%
1. All steps from >5% threshold, plus:
2. New PO issuance suspended pending CAPA completion
3. VP Procurement notified; alternative supplier sourcing initiated
4. Supplier has 45 days to demonstrate improvement below 5%
5. Failure to improve: contract termination process initiated

### Delivery Performance Below 85%
1. Root cause meeting with supplier logistics team within 5 business days
2. Backup supplier activated for 30% of order volume
3. If not improved within 60 days: primary supplier status revoked

## 6. Reporting

- Monthly scorecards distributed to suppliers via supplier portal
- Quarterly trend reports presented to Supply Chain Leadership
- Annual supplier awards for top performers (Score > 4.5 for 12 consecutive months)
