# Emergency Procurement Standard Operating Procedure

**Document ID:** SOP-PROC-003  
**Effective Date:** February 15, 2025  
**Review Cycle:** Annual  
**Owner:** Supply Chain Operations

## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines the conditions and procedures for bypassing standard procurement processes during emergencies. It ensures continuity of supply while maintaining appropriate controls and documentation.

## 2. Emergency Definitions

### Level 1 — Critical Supply Disruption
- Stockout of any product with fewer than 3 days of supply across ALL warehouse regions
- Complete supplier failure (bankruptcy, facility shutdown, regulatory action)
- Trigger: Automatic via inventory monitoring system or manual by Regional Warehouse Manager

### Level 2 — Public Health Emergency
- Declared pandemic, epidemic, or mass casualty event
- Government-mandated supply requirements or allocation orders
- Trigger: VP Operations or above, in coordination with Regulatory Affairs

### Level 3 — Regional Disruption
- Natural disaster affecting a single warehouse region
- Transportation disruption lasting > 48 hours
- Trigger: Regional Warehouse Manager with Procurement Manager approval

## 3. Bypassed Controls

During an active emergency, the following standard controls may be bypassed:

### Three-Quote Requirement
- **Standard process:** Minimum 3 competitive quotes for orders > $10,000
- **Emergency:** Single-source permitted with post-facto justification documented within 72 hours
- **Limit:** Single-source purchases capped at $250,000 per supplier per emergency event

### Supplier Tier Restrictions
- **Standard process:** Tier-3 suppliers limited to $50,000 total POs
- **Emergency:** Tier-3 limit raised to $150,000; Tier-2 suppliers may be used without completed on-site audit
- **Requirement:** Expedited quality inspection on first shipment (within 24 hours of receipt)

### Approval Hierarchy
- **Standard process:** VP approval for orders > $100,000
- **Emergency Level 1:** Procurement Manager may approve up to $200,000
- **Emergency Level 2:** VP Operations may approve up to $1,000,000
- **Emergency Level 3:** Procurement Manager may approve up to $100,000

## 4. Mandatory Controls (Never Bypassed)

Even during emergencies, the following controls remain in force:
1. All products must meet FDA labeling and safety requirements
2. Supplier must provide a Certificate of Analysis (CoA) for pharmaceutical products
3. All transactions must be logged in the ERP system within 24 hours
4. Post-emergency audit required within 30 days of emergency closure
5. No purchases from suppliers on the FDA Debarment List or OFAC sanctions list

## 5. Documentation Requirements

For every emergency procurement action:
- Emergency Declaration Form (signed by authorized trigger role)
- Justification memo for each bypassed control
- Post-event reconciliation report (within 30 days)
- Lessons learned review (within 60 days, presented to Supply Chain Leadership)

## 6. Emergency Supplier List

Procurement maintains a pre-vetted Emergency Supplier List (ESL) updated quarterly. ESL suppliers have:
- Pre-negotiated emergency pricing (within 15% of standard rates)
- Guaranteed 48-hour delivery capability
- Minimum Tier-2 compliance status
- Active NDA and quality agreement on file
