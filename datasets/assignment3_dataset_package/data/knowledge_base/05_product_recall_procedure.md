# Defective Product Recall Procedure

**Document ID:** POL-RCL-005  
**Effective Date:** April 1, 2025  
**Review Cycle:** Semi-Annual  
**Owner:** Quality Assurance & Regulatory Affairs

## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines the workflow for identifying, containing, and resolving defective products in the MedSupply Corp supply chain. It covers voluntary recalls, FDA-mandated recalls, and internal quality holds.

## 2. Recall Classification

### Class I — Dangerous or Defective
Products that could cause serious health consequences or death. Examples: contaminated medications, faulty life-support equipment, mislabeled allergens.
- **Response time:** Initiate within 24 hours of identification
- **Notification:** FDA, all affected customers, warehouse teams, legal counsel
- **Scope:** All units from affected lots across all regions

### Class II — Potentially Harmful  
Products that may cause temporary or medically reversible health consequences. Examples: incorrect dosage labeling, non-sterile items in sterile packaging.
- **Response time:** Initiate within 48 hours
- **Notification:** FDA (within 10 business days), affected customers
- **Scope:** Affected lots identified through traceability system

### Class III — Not Likely Harmful
Products unlikely to cause adverse health consequences but violate FDA regulations. Examples: minor labeling errors, cosmetic defects.
- **Response time:** Initiate within 5 business days
- **Notification:** FDA as required, affected customers at next regular communication
- **Scope:** Targeted lot-level recall

## 3. Identification & Escalation

Defective products may be identified through:
1. Customer complaint (logged in CRM within 4 hours of receipt)
2. Internal quality inspection finding
3. Supplier notification of manufacturing defect
4. FDA safety alert or MedWatch report
5. Post-market surveillance data analysis

**Escalation Matrix:**
- Single complaint: Quality Specialist investigates (SLA: 48 hours)
- 3+ complaints on same lot: Quality Manager notified, lot placed on hold
- Pattern across multiple lots: VP Quality and Regulatory Affairs notified, recall evaluation initiated
- Any life-threatening report: Immediate escalation to VP Quality, Regulatory Affairs, and Legal

## 4. Quarantine Process

Once a recall is initiated:
1. All affected lots in all warehouses placed on HOLD status in the inventory system (within 2 hours)
2. Physical isolation: products moved to quarantine area with restricted access
3. Shipping hold: any in-transit shipments containing affected lots flagged for intercept or return
4. Customer notification with return instructions (pre-paid shipping labels generated automatically)

**For recalls affecting more than 1,000 units:**
- Dedicated recall coordinator assigned
- Daily status reports to VP Quality
- Customer communication escalated to include phone calls (not just email)
- Press/media statement prepared by Communications team (standby unless public inquiry received)

## 5. Root Cause Analysis

Within 30 days of recall initiation:
- 8D problem-solving methodology applied
- Supplier CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action) required within 15 business days
- If supplier fails to provide adequate CAPA: supplier placed on probation (Tier downgrade consideration)
- Findings documented in Recall Report and shared with Supply Chain Leadership

## 6. Regulatory Reporting

- FDA MedWatch Form 3500A submitted for all Class I and Class II recalls
- Quarterly recall summary report to FDA (if applicable)
- Annual recall metrics included in Management Review
- All documentation retained for minimum 10 years
