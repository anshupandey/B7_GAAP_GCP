# Supplier Onboarding Policy

**Document ID:** POL-SUP-001  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Review Cycle:** Annual  
**Owner:** Procurement & Compliance Division

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the standard procedure for onboarding new suppliers into the MedSupply Corp procurement ecosystem. All new suppliers must complete the full onboarding workflow before any purchase orders can be issued.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all categories: PPE, Pharmaceuticals, Medical Equipment, and Consumables. Applies globally across all warehouse regions (US-East, US-West, US-Central, EU-West).

## 3. Compliance Tier Definitions

### Tier-1 (FDA-Approved)
- Holds active FDA registration for all supplied product categories
- Has passed an on-site audit within the last 18 months
- Maintains ISO 13485 certification (Medical Devices Quality Management)
- Defect rate below 3% over trailing 12 months
- Minimum 2 years of supply chain track record

### Tier-2 (Audit-Pending)
- FDA registration application submitted or under review
- Passed a remote document audit but on-site audit pending
- ISO 13485 certification in progress or holds ISO 9001 as interim
- Defect rate below 7% over trailing 12 months
- Probationary period: 6 months with monthly performance reviews

### Tier-3 (New Supplier)
- No FDA registration; regulatory compliance must be verified within 90 days
- Requires a sponsor from the Procurement team and VP approval
- Limited to purchase orders under $50,000 total until promoted to Tier-2
- Mandatory quality inspection on first 3 shipments (100% inspection rate)
- Maximum contract duration: 6 months; renewal requires Tier-2 promotion

## 4. Required Documents for Onboarding

All suppliers must submit the following before onboarding approval:

1. Certificate of Incorporation or Business Registration
2. FDA registration certificate (Tier-1) or application receipt (Tier-2)
3. ISO 13485 or ISO 9001 certification
4. Product catalog with specifications and material safety data sheets (MSDS)
5. Bank details and W-9 (US) or W-8BEN (international) tax forms
6. References from at least 2 existing healthcare customers
7. Insurance certificate: minimum $2M product liability coverage
8. Signed MedSupply Corp Supplier Code of Conduct

## 5. Approval Workflow

1. Procurement Specialist submits onboarding request with all documents
2. Compliance team reviews documents (SLA: 5 business days)
3. Quality Assurance schedules initial product sample testing (SLA: 10 business days)
4. Finance verifies banking and tax documentation (SLA: 3 business days)
5. Final approval by Procurement Manager (Tier-2/3) or VP Procurement (Tier-1)
6. Supplier record created in ERP; assigned compliance tier

## 6. Exceptions

Emergency suppliers (activated under the Emergency Procurement SOP) may begin receiving POs before full onboarding is complete, but must complete all documentation within 30 days. Failure to complete results in automatic suspension.
